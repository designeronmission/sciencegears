1. Introduction

Welcome to the Sciencegears Potentiostat user manual. This advanced electrochemical instrument is designed for researchers and engineers working in fields such as corrosion science, battery development, and sensor research.

Quick Start: For immediate use, connect electrodes, launch the software, and select your experiment type from the template library.

Key Features

  • Precision voltage and current measurement
  • Multiple electrochemical techniques (CV, EIS, LSV, etc.)
  • High-speed data acquisition
  • User-friendly software interface

2. Safety Information

DANGER: High voltage hazard. This instrument can generate potentially lethal voltages. Always disconnect power before making any connections.
WARNING: Chemical hazard. Many electrochemical experiments involve corrosive or toxic solutions. Always use appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE).

Safety Guidelines

  • Always work in a properly ventilated area
  • Wear safety goggles and gloves when handling chemicals
  • Ensure proper grounding of all equipment
  • Do not operate with wet hands or in damp environments
  • Keep flammable materials away from the work area

3. Technical Specifications

Parameter Specification
Voltage Range ±10V
Current Range 1nA to 100mA (8 ranges)
Input Impedance >1TΩ
Potential Resolution 300µV
Current Resolution 0.3% of range
Supported Techniques CV, LSV, NPV, DPV, SWV, EIS, CA, CP
Interface USB 2.0/3.0, Ethernet (optional)
Note: Specifications may vary slightly between models. Refer to your instrument's calibration certificate for exact values.

4. Hardware Setup

Connecting the Electrodes

  1. Connect the working electrode (red connector) to your sample
  2. Connect the reference electrode (green connector) to your reference (e.g., Ag/AgCl)
  3. Connect the counter electrode (black connector) to your counter electrode (e.g., platinum wire)
  4. Ensure all connections are secure before powering on
Important: Always connect the reference electrode last and disconnect it first to avoid damaging the instrument.
